Our school is the culmination of a vision sparked in 1978 by Professor Ajaga Nji during his studies in the United States. His lifelong commitment to transformative education led to groundbreaking contributions, including the creation of Cameroon’s first distance education program—now recognized as modern online education—and the establishment of the Higher National Diploma Program (HND) during his tenure as Inspector General at the Ministry of Higher Education in Cameroon. Recognizing that sustainable change begins at the grassroots, Professor Nji envisioned a solid foundation through elementary and kindergarten education, which became the cornerstone of our school’s mission.


We honor the enduring legacy of our beloved founder, whose spirit continues to inspire our mission to educate, empower, and uplift future generations. His extensive writings, including Why Poor People Remain Poor, The Triumph of Social Democracy, Understanding the Politics of Jesus, and As I Saw It, encapsulate his vision for inclusive, equitable, and progressive societies. In As I Saw It, Professor Nji inspires the youth, particularly in resource-poor settings, to persevere and recognize their potential to shape and be shaped by society. By purchasing these books, you directly support our mission, as each sale funds the education of at least one child, fostering community development and sustainable progress.


Our school is not a fleeting idea but a journey rooted in nearly five decades of dedication to education and social change. Through each child’s success, we carry forward Professor Nji’s legacy with integrity, compassion, and purpose, ensuring his dream of a brighter future endures.
